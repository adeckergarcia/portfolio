About Me

The Journey

As a kid, I wanted to be an olympic gymnast, a lawyer, and a broadway star. As time went on, I thought maybe I’d be a teacher, an event planner, or even the CEO of a symphony. Big dreams, all over the map.

When I look back, I can remember exactly why I wanted to do each of these things. Sometimes, it was because I wanted to entertain or be creative. Other times, I wanted to help people and make a difference in their lives.

In each new job I tried, I found myself taking on creative responsibilities that weren’t always in my job description. I enjoyed doing those things, but it made me realize something was missing.

The Spark

In late 2017, I became engaged to my now husband. He had just enrolled in a coding bootcamp to become a software engineer, and when we decided to have a wedding website for our guests, he suggested we try to build it ourselves. Neither of us had ever done anything like that before, but we gave it a try.

To our surprise, we pieced together something that we were actually pretty proud of.

Designing that wedding website was the spark I needed to rethink my career. I wanted to learn more about the people who got to do this in real life, and that’s when I discovered Product Design. I felt like I found the holy grail: an opportunity to be creative and help people, dig into the technology we use every day, and understand how to make it better.

I quit the job I wasn’t thrilled about, signed up for Bloc’s Product Design program, and here we are.

The Why

So what do I love about product design? Every day, I learn something new. I love building something from scratch and producing real solutions for real people. I love figuring out why something didn’t work and learning from those mistakes. I love the opportunity we have to impact people’s lives through great design, which is so much more than a beautiful interface.

And, I get *really* excited about usability tests.
